Throughout my time here at Mizzou there has been lots I have explored both physically, mentally, socially, and figuratively. The knowledge I’ve gained didn’t come naturally and it took a lot of networking to learn the things I do now. I’ve missed out on a lot, even nearing the end of my 5th year of school there are different communities and areas of both campus and Columbia as a whole, that I have yet to see for myself and I would hate for others to be without the knowledge and opportunities to make their experience as best as it can be. The goal of this is to give people the opportunity to have a much more centralized and reliably informative source to find the org or business for themselves. The site will feature black student business, things to do and places to go around Columbia, black student organizations, and other campus-related resources that students may not know about if they haven’t been told about it.

I am 5th-year Senior here at the University of Missouri and I will be graduating with a Degree in Bachelors of General Studies with emphasis’ in Information Technology, Psychology, and Business; in addition to a Multicultural Certificate, Media Technology Design Certificate, and an Information Systems Certificate. I have held several positions of leadership on campus in organizations such as the National Society of Black Engineers, United Ambassadors Diversity Recruitment Team, THRIVE Student Community of Practice, the Legion of Black Collegians, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., and University of Missouri National Pan-Hellenic Council. As you can see I have had my hand in a lot of different areas, thus showing even if I personally may not know something I can ask someone in one my networks for suggestions and answers. I have a thirst for knowledge and an even more intense drive to helping others and I strive to add to both of those each day.

Here in this sentence, I will link my Resume for viewings of my credentials.
